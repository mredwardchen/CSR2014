ASE/TDK JV places embedded technology processes under one roof

by Debra Vogler, Senior Technical Editor


William T. Chen, ASE Fellow and Senior Technical Advisor

Advanced Semiconductor Engineering, Inc. (ASE) recently announced that it will enter into a joint venture agreement with TDK Corporation to establish a joint venture company in Taiwan. The JV company – to be called ASE Embedded Electronics Inc. – is expected to manufacture IC embedded substrates utilizing TDK's SESUB technology (Figure 1). Additionally, provisions in the various agreements may allow for the JV company to take on a part of TDK's production capacity for IC embedded substrates under certain specific circumstances. The JV Company's factory and manufacturing facility are expected to be located in the Nantze Export Processing Zone, Kaohsiung City, Taiwan. The proposed establishment of and capital injection into the joint venture company is subject to various regulatory approvals or consents (including but not limited to the approvals by the Taiwan Fair Trade Commission and the Export Processing Zone Administration).

According to William T. Chen, ASE Fellow and Senior Technical Advisor, the projected timeline for establishment of the JV company is as follows (subject to local agency reviews): 1) Technology transfer, set-up of the factory, and qualification will take place from mid-2015 to about 1Q’16; 2) Production ramp-up is expected to start by 2Q’16. More specifics about the schedule will not be available until the technology teams have had a chance to work together, Chen told Chip Scale Review.

Figure 1: The graphic illustrates the 60% reduction in size that is a result of TDK’s SESUB technology. SOURCE: TDK Corporation

John Hunt, Director of Engineering, Product Promotion at ASE, told CSR that the JV enables the capability of doing complete front-to-back processing. “Previously, most of the individual process steps used in embedded technologies were done by discrete companies, with parts being shipped around the world,” said Hunt. “With this JV, we have the total capability to do front-to-back the entire process all the way up to assembly. So from bumping, to substrate, to the assembly – this is the first time we have been able to integrate all of these processes under one roof.”

ASE will be using TDK’s material set—using copy exactly methodology. “TDK optimized the process and materials set for high yields,” explained Hunt. “It uses tried and true technology with structures that has been proven in production to give high yields.”

Because many of the target applications for the new product series are new or emerging fields, including silicon photonics, much of the work involved in heterogeneous materials integration is taking place today at the R&D level at universities and research institutes, Uhrmann told CSR. “As a result, we are offering an entry-level tool configuration that supports R&D research in these areas,” said Uhrmann. “The entry-level tool configuration has the same CAM module design inherent in the standard and HVM configurations, but comes with one cassette station or manual load port, as well as a single-arm robot to support up to three process modules, which is sufficient for research needs.”

ASE’s release noted that the initial paid-in capital amount for the JV company will be the equivalent in NT dollars of US$39,490,000. ASE will inject US$20,140,000 into the JV company for a 51% shareholding, and TDK will inject US$19,350,000 into the JV company for a 49% shareholding. After the JV company obtains agreement from ASE and TDK to begin construction of its first manufacturing line, the JV company will pay US$19,350,000 as a down payment for the royalty fees for the SESUB technology license granted by TDK.
