Industry Events - 2015

November 2015


SEMICON Europa 2015: a great place to meet

by Steffen Kroehnert [NANIUM S.A.]

Visitors from around the world once again flocked to Dresden, Germany, to attend SEMICON Europa 2015 (October 6-8). The conference attracted visitors from major microelectronics and electronic systems companies, including buying teams from the world’s top capital spending IDMs, foundries, and fabless companies. The event has proven to be the largest semiconductor event in Europe with nearly 5,000 industry experts, professionals and executives attending. Compared to SEMICON Europa 2013 in Dresden, the 2015 show saw a 30% increase in exhibition space, a 10% increase in show attendance, and a 20% increase in attendance at the Innovation Village (Figure 1) — a hub for start-ups, investors and innovators. There were also new programs such as: Electronics for Automotive, MedTech Driven by Semiconductors, and Industry 4.0 — Microelectronics as enabler for advanced factory automation. Over 40 media partners (Chip Scale Review being one of them) and 28 institutions supported the event.

Figure 1: : Presentations at Innovation Village. SOURCE: SEMI

  The show program was rich in content ‑ with 250 speakers in over 70 conferences, sessions and workshops ‑ and attended by approximately 1,000 attendees in total. The exhibition floor was quite busy most of the time and featured 360 exhibiting companies showcasing recent developments on more than 4,000m2 of floor space with much more hardware, equipment and tools than in the last few years, by my observation. Besides wanting to learn new things, it was clearly evident that people are coming to this event mainly to meet and to network. Representatives of many companies — and not necessarily all exhibitors — were there, running individual meetings on site. Even the evenings went late into the night with networking events, such as the “SAXONY! – Get Together” event that took place on Tuesday evening in the Royal Palace in Dresden’s fascinating ancient city. The event was hosted by the Saxony State Ministry for Economic Affairs, Labor and Transport and supported by Saxony Economic Development Corporation, Saxony’s State Capital Dresden, and SEMI Europe.

Packaging events at the show

Figure 4: : Advanced Packaging Conference (APC): Rozalia Beica, CTO, Yole Développement, presents keynote address: “The growth of advanced packaging: an overview of technologies, applications and market trends.” SOURCE: SEMI

The theme for this year’s Advanced Packaging Conference (APC) was “Interconnects in Miniaturized Systems.” The conference also included posters and a panel session, which was well attended (92 registrations). There was a lot of constructive interaction with the audience. As co-chair of the APC committee, along with Andy Longford (PandA Europe), I can say that one of the reasons it was decided to talk about interconnects in miniaturized systems was because of the wide range of applications from consumer to automotive and power products, lighting, sensors and photonics, that are driving system integration in the “More-than-Moore” domain and require advanced packaging to accommodate further miniaturization and performance enhancement. The package ‑ with its electrical, thermal and mechanical properties ‑ becomes an important part of the system functionality. What did not change with this development is the utmost importance of interconnects as the main feature of semiconductor packages. The APC keynotes were given by Bernd Roemer, Senior Principal, Infineon Technologies AG: “Innovative interconnects in system-in-package drive application,” and Rozalia Beica, CTO, Yole Développement: “The growth of advanced packaging: an overview of technologies, applications, and market trends” (see Figures 2 and 3).

  On Wednesday afternoon of show week, the European Semiconductor Packaging, Assembly and Test (ESPAT) industry interest group met with about 30 participants. The main topic was the kick-off of its transition into a special interest group (SIG) under SEMI’s “umbrella.” That move was decided during the last ESPAT meeting in June of this year, which was hosted by NANIUM in Portugal. ESPAT is now ESiPAT Group (European SEMI integrated Packaging Assembly and Test). The first two projects that were started are related to the setup of a directory about semiconductor packaging, assembly and test players in Europe, their capabilities and capacities, and the initiative to apply for a European Advanced Packaging and Test Pilot Line within the ECSEL Joint Undertaking (Electronic Components and Systems for European Leadership) program.  

  Finally, at the SEMI members’ breakfast on Thursday morning of show week, Laith Altimime, the new president of SEMI Europe (as of October 1, 2015), presented himself to the members. Replacing Heinz Kundert, he will lead SEMI’s activities in Europe, the Middle East, and North Africa.
