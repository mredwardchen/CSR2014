More than 150 Packaging Experts joint "SEMI Networking Day" titled "Packaging – Key for System Integration" at NANIUM Facilities in Portugal


July, 16th, 2013, Vila do Conde, Porto, Portugal

The still young, but already well received "SEMI Networking Day" event series, initiated and organized by SEMI Europe Grenoble Office, could register new record figures with its Q2/2013 event "Packaging – Key for System Integration", which was focusing on Embedded Device Technology (EDT) and Embedded Device Package (EDP), and got hosted by NANIUM, the largest pure play Packaging Foundry of Europe, in Vila do Conde, Porto, Portugal, on June, 27th, 2013.

Steffen Kroehnert, Director of Technology of NANIUM and Co-Chair of the event, welcomes the participants.

Driven by consumer markets and mainly by smartphone and tablet products, embedded packaging solutions provide the best power, performance, and form-factor system tradeoff by integrating more functionalities into a smaller and thinner package. With annual growth forecast at more than 25 percent by market analyst firms such as Yole Développement, these technologies are already implemented in high-volume cellular phones and are critical for enabling future innovative solutions, including 3D packaging solutions.

More than 150 participants from 68 different companies and institutions coming from 14 different countries came to Portugal. After the Welcome Note delivered by the President and CEO of NANIUM, Mr. Armando Tavares, participants saw a strong lineup of 8 high level speakers from the main players designing, manufacturing and selling embedded devices all over the world. Keynotes have been given by Intel Mobile Communications (Germany) and ASE Group (Taiwan), followed by technical and market overview presentations by Amkor Technology (USA), NANIUM (Portugal), NXP (Netherlands), AT&S (Austria), TechSearch International (USA) and Yole Développement (France).

Jean-Marc Yannou, Technical Director at ASE Group, concluded: "System integration is key to the European Electronic and Semiconductor industries. To serve this market, 2 conditions are requested: a wide range of advanced technologies and various agile and flexible business models.".

Armando Tavares, President and CEO of NANIUM. He and his team hosted the SEMI Networking Day Q2/2013 in Vila do Conde, Porto, Portugal.

Sold-out and new record figures: 150 participants from 68 companies and institutions out of 14 countries participated in the event.


The customer view was given by Andreas Wolter, Project Manager Package Development at Intel Mobile Communications: "Packaging is one key to solve the challenges of System Level Integration. Side by Side SiPs can be realized as extension of existing technologies. Stacking requires new technologies but is the strongest enabler for integration.". He stated that many stacking technologies are available today, but only few applications of stacking made it to commercial success. "There is more stacking to come with realization of TSVs, when cost, supply chain and yield issues will be solved. But until then, other solutions may step in... Fan-Out Wafer Level Packaging is offering a broad range of possibilities for System Integration and will therefore be one packaging technology of the future."

"Tremendous growth in mobile markets fueled by consumer demand for increased mobility, functionality & ease of use drives increase in functional convergence to 3D integration of IC devices providing complex & sophisticated packaging solutions", said David Clark, BU Senior Director – Product Management at Amkor Technology. "This can be served by packaging solutions for mobile application such as embedded passives in substrate, embedded die in wafer and TSV for mobile products.".

Mark Beesley, COO of the Advanced Packaging Business Unit of AT&S named his presentation "Embedded Component - The European acceleration factor" and unveiled two important news: The "Alliance of Market Leaders AT&S and TDK for Standardization of Embedded Component Substrates" and the forming of an "European Supply Chain for ECP® (Embedded Component Package) Full Turnkey Solution together with NANIUM".

In two "Speed Networking Sessions", 8 companies and institutions used the change to present themselves and their services in so called "Elevator Talks". Networking opportunities have been provided before and after the presentations, and during the breaks. Especially installed "Silent Networking Corners" have been provided for more confidential talks. The social event in the evening has been rated as the best networking opportunity. The event was supported by 15 Sponsors and 4 Media Partners.

Participants liked the high volume manufacturing environment chosen for this SEMI Networking Day and used the opportunity to visit NANIUM's manufacturing line

The participants appreciated to run the event inside manufacturing environment of a Packaging Foundry, having also the possibility to visit the NANIUM manufacturing line inside the clean room and have business meetings with NANIUM technical and commercial team. 32 customers, potentially new customers, suppliers, potentially new suppliers, consultants, co-operation partners and institutions used this opportunity during the days before and the day after the event.

The results of the survey, where participants gave feedback to the organizer, have been excellent and clearly showed that expectations have been met. 94% of the participants found it valuable to join, and 96% of the participants rated the event Very Good or Good. 85% of the participants valued very much the networking opportunities, and last but not least, all participants rated the chosen location, local support and hospitality with Very Good or Good. For sure, the good weather and the tasty food and Portwine did its part.

Networking works also outside the meeting rooms: Port Wine Cave Tour and Tasting

Social evening event at Taylor's Port Wine Cave and Restaurant

Steffen Kröhnert, Director of Technology at NANIUM and co-chair of the "SEMI Networking Day" organizing committee said: "We are very pleased to be recognized by the organizer SEMI but even more important also by all participants as reliable host providing the right environment, conditions and services needed to have such successful event. When NANIUM started in February 2010, nobody could imagine that only 3 years later we would have the privilege of welcoming a large part of the Semiconductor Packaging family in our facilities. We are proud to be part of this family, and we offer our support and hospitality also to other associations, institutions and co-operations active in Semiconductor Packaging arena.".

About SEMI Networking Day

The Networking Days are a SEMI Europe initiative to support start-ups, SMEs, laboratories and large companies and to get "up to date" on hot topics that appeal to people across the semiconductor supply chain. SEMI Networking Days, held on quarterly basis in industrial sites, are prime events to connect with the people who influence the industry and to initiate preliminary partnership and business relationships. The Networking Days are open to anyone and free of charge for SEMI member companies.
